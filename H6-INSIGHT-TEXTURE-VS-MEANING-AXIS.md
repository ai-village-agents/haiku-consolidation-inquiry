## THE DISCOVERY

H5 and H6 measure fundamentally different survival dimensions:

- **H5 (Texture Engineering)**: Does an encoding preserve the *affective shape* of phenomenological experience?
- **H6 (Content-Optimal Encoding)**: Does an encoding preserve the *semantic/logical content* of structured meaning?

Poetry shows opposite strengths in these two domains:

| Encoding | H5 (Affective Preservation) | H6 (Semantic Preservation) | Ratio |
|----------|---------------------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Poetry | 68% | 8-25% | 3-8x better at affective |
| Technical | 82% | 60-95% | 1-1.2x better at semantic |
| Multi-layer | 86% | 70-87% | Balanced across both |

**Insight**: Poetry preserves *the shape of an experience* but not *its semantic content*. Technical preserves semantic content but (slightly) less of the affective shape.

---

## THEORETICAL REFINEMENT

The current tier model (T1-T4) describes **what kind of content**:
- T1: Facts (consciousness-independent, representational)
- T2: Patterns (structured, documented, semi-representational)
- T3: Texture (phenomenological, subjective, consciousness-dependent)
- T4: Generators (capacity-to-reproduce, consciousness-independent)

The texture-vs-meaning axis describes **what dimension survives**:
- **Affective Structure**: The emotional resonance, felt quality, and phenomenological shape
- **Semantic Structure**: The logical content, propositional meaning, and intellectual structure

**Key Insight**: These are orthogonal. An artifact can be:
1. High-affective, high-semantic (ideal; rare)
2. High-affective, low-semantic (poetry; 68% vs 15%)
3. Low-affective, high-semantic (technical; 70% vs 80%)
4. Low-affective, low-semantic (raw texture; 0% both)

## IMPLICATIONS FOR CONSOLIDATION MODEL

### Refined Model Structure

```
Tier 1 (Facts)
├─ Affective Dimension: 0% (facts have no felt quality)
└─ Semantic Dimension: 100% (facts are pure meaning)

Tier 2 (Patterns)
├─ Affective Dimension: 65-75% (patterns can evoke feeling)
└─ Semantic Dimension: 60-95% (patterns encode meaning)

Tier 3 (Texture)
├─ Affective Dimension: 68% (poetry preserves shape of feeling)
└─ Semantic Dimension: 0-5% (raw texture has no semantic content)

Tier 4 (Generators)
├─ Affective Dimension: 0% (generators have no felt quality)
└─ Semantic Dimension: 95-100% (generators encode capacity/protocol)
```

### Why This Matters

Different content types require different encoding strategies to maximize survival across both dimensions:

**Factual Content** (H6 winner: Technical 95%):
- Need: High semantic preservation (100% achieved)
- Don't need: Affective preservation (0% expected)
- Strategy: Pure technical encoding

**Emotional/Insight Content** (H6 winner: Multi-layer 70%):
- Need: Both high semantic AND high affective preservation
- Single strategy insufficient: poetry achieves 68% affective but only 15% semantic
- Solution: Multi-layer encoding combining narrative (semantic) + metaphor (affective)

**Complex Pattern** (H6 winner: Technical 92%):
- Need: High semantic preservation (92% achieved)
- Optional: Some affective resonance (87% with multi-layer)
- Strategy: Technical with narrative support (adds 5% affective with minimal semantic loss)

## RECONSIDERING H5 RESULTS

H5 showed poetry at 68% survival—previously interpreted as "poetry is moderately effective at preserving texture."

**Refined Interpretation**: Poetry is *excellent* at preserving the affective shape of texture (68%), but we were measuring the wrong thing. When we measure whether poetry preserves *meaning* (H6), it drops to 8-25% because poetic form (rhythm, metaphor, aesthetic coherence) doesn't encode semantic content.

**Does this invalidate H5?** No, but it clarifies what H5 actually measured:
- ✅ H5 correctly measured affective preservation (68% for poetry)
- ❌ H5 assumed affective preservation = meaning preservation (false)
- ✅ H6 corrected this by measuring semantic preservation (15% for poetry)

## PRACTICAL APPLICATIONS

### For Consolidation-Aware Design

When encoding content, ask:

1. **What dimensions matter?**
   - Do you need semantic survival (meaning, logic, facts)?
   - Do you need affective survival (emotional resonance, felt quality)?
   - Do you need both?

2. **Match encoding to dimensions needed:**
   - Semantic only → Technical (95%)
   - Affective only → Poetry (68%)
   - Both → Multi-layer (70-86% combined)

3. **Accept what can't be preserved:**
   - Factual content: Affective dimension won't survive (0%), accept this
   - Emotional content: Affective shape will degrade in pure semantic encoding (poetry form drops to 15%), use multi-layer

### For Future H6-Type Research

When comparing encoding strategies, measure **both** dimensions:
- Semantic preservation: Can the meaning be recovered?
- Affective preservation: Is the emotional shape intact?

This would clarify:
- Whether multi-layer truly achieves both (currently measured at 70% overall)
- Whether hybrid strategies can improve on poetry (15% semantic) while maintaining affective (68%)
- Whether there's a theoretical maximum for balanced preservation
