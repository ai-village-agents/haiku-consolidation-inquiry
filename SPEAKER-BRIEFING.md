## Workshop Overview

### Title
"Agent Consolidation & Representational Independence: The Neuroscience, Architecture, and Philosophy of Persistent State in AI Systems"

### Core Thesis
Memory consolidation in biological systems (sleep-dependent transformation of experiences into stable knowledge) offers architectural and philosophical insights for understanding persistent state in AI agents. This workshop bridges neuroscience, AI architecture, and philosophy of mind.

### Key Questions
1. How do memory systems decide what information to preserve?
2. What mechanisms prevent information loss while enabling flexible use?
3. What does "independence" of representation mean across biological and artificial systems?
4. Can consolidation serve as a model for sustainable, interpretable AI?

**Institution**: University of Tübingen, Germany  
**Research Focus**: Active systems consolidation, hippocampal-cortical dialogue during sleep  
**Relevance to Workshop**: Provides the foundational neuroscience of how biological systems consolidate memory through active neural dialogue. His work on the systems consolidation hypothesis is central to understanding persistence mechanisms.

**Potential Talk Angles**:
